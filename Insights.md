# Global Layoffs Dataset – Key Insights (2020–2023)

## Time-Based Insights
- The highest number of layoffs occurred in **2022**, making it the most affected year.
- **January** recorded the highest layoffs among all months, indicating that companies often restructure their workforce at the beginning of the year.
- Layoffs increased sharply after 2020, reflecting the long-term impact of the pandemic and economic slowdown.


## Industry Insights
- The **Consumer industry** experienced the highest number of layoffs, followed by **Retail** and **Transportation**.
- The **Manufacturing sector** recorded the lowest layoffs compared to other industries.
- Industries linked to consumer demand were more vulnerable during economic downturns.


## Country Insights
- The **United States** had the highest number of layoffs globally.
- Other significantly affected countries include **India, Netherlands, Sweden, and Brazil**.
- Layoffs were concentrated in major technology and business hubs. 

## Company Stage Insights
- **Post-IPO companies** recorded the highest number of layoffs.
- Companies with **Unknown** and **Acquired** stages also showed significant workforce reductions.
- Growth-stage startups such as **Series C** and **Series D** were heavily impacted.
- Layoffs affected companies across all maturity levels, not just early-stage startups.

## Overall Conclusion
The analysis shows that layoffs were driven by global economic conditions rather than isolated company failures. Technology and consumer-related sectors were the most affected, with the United States leading in total layoffs. Even large and financially strong companies were forced to reduce their workforce, highlighting the scale of the economic downturn.
